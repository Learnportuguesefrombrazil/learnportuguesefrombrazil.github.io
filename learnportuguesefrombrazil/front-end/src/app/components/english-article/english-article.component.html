Top Tips for Choosing the Right Holiday Restaurant

Do your research

Have a look at review websites ‚Äî or even better, just ask local people. If you're staying at a hotel, the staff should be able to recommend a good place to go.

Look at the menu

According to Jay Rayner, a food writer for the Observer, you should look at how long the menu is and the words it uses.

He says that while it's fine if there are five to six main meals on the menu, a long menu could mean a restaurant is using more frozen food.

A shorter menu might mean they have perfected a small number of great dishes. Rayner also believes too many adjectives in a menu mean the restaurant is trying too hard with its words, and not trying hard enough in its kitchen!

Most of us will look at the prices on the menu, but if you like a glass of wine, don't forget to check those prices as well, says Rayner.

Some restaurants have a food menu that doesn't look too expensive, but a wine list where the cheapest bottle is not cheap at all!

You'll get a good idea of what a restaurant is like by looking in the window. But if staff run out and try to pull you inside with deals and promises ‚Äî you might not be in the best place!
