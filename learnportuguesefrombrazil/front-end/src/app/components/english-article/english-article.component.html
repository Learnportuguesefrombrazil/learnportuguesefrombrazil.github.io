Brazil Reintroduces Visas for Japanese Tourists

From October 1, Brazil will start requiring citizens of the US, Australia, Canada, and Japan to obtain tourist visas in order to enter the country.

In 2019, former president Jair Bolsonaro removed visa requirements for those countries in order to promote Brazil's tourism industry. However, the US, Australia, Canada and Japan still demanded visas from Brazilian tourists.

A foreign ministry statement said that Brazil's migration policy was historically based on "equal treatment." The ministry added that the government is ready to negotiate new visa agreements on a reciprocal basis.

Bolsonaro criticized the decision last week, writing on Twitter that it would lead to "Less jobs and less stimulus of the hotel sector."
