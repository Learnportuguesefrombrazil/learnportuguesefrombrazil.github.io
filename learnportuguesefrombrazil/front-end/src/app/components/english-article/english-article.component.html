Short Bursts of Activity May Lower Risk of Death

Older people who do several short bursts of physical activity each day have a lower risk of early death, researchers have found.

A team of international scientists said that doing just three or four minutes of vigorous activity a day can cut the risk of early death, compared with doing no vigorous activity.

Writing for the website The Conversation, several of the researchers explained that this vigorous activity included normal events in people's daily lives, such as playing with children, carrying shopping, walking up steps, or running to catch a train.

The researchers looked at data from about 14,000 women and 11,000 men who were part of the UK Biobank study, with an average age of 62. About 60% of the participants walked once a week or less, but they did no other exercise.

The participants wore fitness trackers to count their physical activity for one week. The researchers also studied their medical records over about seven years.

They found that people who had three bursts of vigorous activity daily, each lasting one or two minutes, lowered their risk of death from all causes by up to 40%. The risk of death from heart disease was reduced by up to 49%.

The results are "striking," and come after other studies found that short bursts of vigorous activity can improve the health of people's hearts and lungs, the researchers wrote.

They also wrote that many people don't exercise enough because they say they don't have time. But it may be easier to motivate people to do a few short bursts of activity each day so they can still get some health benefits.
